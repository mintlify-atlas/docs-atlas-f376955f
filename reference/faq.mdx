  <Accordion title="Why was I logged out automatically?">
    Your session expires after **10 minutes of inactivity**. The system monitors mouse movement, clicks, keyboard input, scrolling, and touch events. If none of these occur for 9 minutes, you will see a warning notification:

    > "Tu sesión cerrará en 1 minuto por inactividad"

    If you remain inactive for the full 10 minutes, you are logged out automatically and redirected to the login screen. Simply log in again to continue working.

    To avoid unexpected logouts, keep interacting with the application normally while you are filling out a document.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="I can't find my area's machotes — what should I do?">
    This usually means your account does not have the correct area assigned. Check the following:

    1. Confirm with your administrator that your account has been assigned the correct area. All users can browse machotes for their assigned area — but if `areasPermitidas` is empty or missing, no machotes will appear.
    2. Ask your administrator to verify that your `areasPermitidas` list includes the correct area ID (for example, `recursos-humanos` or `tesoreria`).
    3. If your `areasPermitidas` is empty and your role is `jefe_area`, contact your system administrator to update your account.

    Administrators always see all nine areas regardless of their `areasPermitidas` setting.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I edit a document after it's been generated?">
    It depends on the document's status:

    - **`borrador` (draft):** You can update the field values using the edit action on the document. The document remains in your history and can be finalized later.
    - **`final`:** The document is considered complete. To make changes, contact your area head (`jefe_area`), who can coordinate with the administrator if needed.

    Changing a document's status from `borrador` to `final` is intentionally irreversible through the standard UI to preserve document integrity.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What file formats are supported for letterheads?">
    The system accepts the following formats for hojas membretadas (letterheads):

    | Format | Extension | Notes |
    |--------|-----------|-------|
    | PNG | `.png` | Recommended — clearest results in generated documents |
    | JPG / JPEG | `.jpg`, `.jpeg` | Recommended — good quality, smaller file size |
    | WebP | `.webp` | Supported |
    | PDF | `.pdf` | Supported for upload; a JPEG preview is generated automatically for display |

    **PNG or JPG provide the clearest results** in the final generated documents. If you upload a PDF, the system stores the original for download but renders a JPEG preview for use inside document templates.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="My machote variables are not appearing as form fields — why?">
    Variables must be written using **square bracket** syntax: `[VariableName]`

    Common mistakes and their fixes:

    | Incorrect | Correct |
    |-----------|---------|
    | `{NombreEmpleado}` | `[NombreEmpleado]` |
    | `(NombreEmpleado)` | `[NombreEmpleado]` |
    | `<NombreEmpleado>` | `[NombreEmpleado]` |
    | `NombreEmpleado` (no brackets) | `[NombreEmpleado]` |

    **Curly brace syntax `{{Variable}}` is supported** and is automatically converted to `[Variable]` when the template is saved. However, single curly braces `{variable}` are only converted if the name starts with an uppercase letter and the braces are not inside a CSS rule or JSON value.

    After correcting the syntax, save the machote. The system re-extracts variables on every save, so the form fields will update immediately.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I deactivate a template without deleting it?">
    Yes. Use the **deactivate** action on the machote (available to `jefe_area` and `administrador` accounts). This sets the machote's status to `inactive` and records a `fechaBaja` timestamp.

    What deactivation does:

    - The machote no longer appears in the list for users generating documents.
    - All previously generated documents remain in the history and are unaffected.
    - The template content, variables, and full audit history are preserved.

    A deactivated machote can be **reactivated** at any time using the reactivate action, which sets the status back to `active` and records a `fechaAlta` timestamp. No data is lost during deactivation or reactivation.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I reset my password?">
    There are two scenarios:

    **First login:** When your account is created, you are assigned an initial password. On your first login you will be prompted to:
    1. Accept the **aviso de privacidad** (privacy notice).
    2. Set a new password to replace the initial one.

    You cannot access the application until both steps are completed.

    **Subsequent resets:** There is no self-service password reset link. Contact your system administrator to reset your password. The administrator can issue a new initial password, which triggers the first-login flow again on your next session.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What happens to documents if a machote is deactivated?">
    Deactivating a machote only affects **future** document generation:

    - **Existing documents** in the history are fully preserved. You can still view, print, and download them.
    - **New documents** cannot be generated from an inactive machote. The machote will not appear in the selection list.

    If you need to generate a new document based on an inactive machote, an administrator or area head must reactivate it first, generate the document, and then deactivate it again if required.
  </Accordion>
